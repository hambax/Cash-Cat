# Who Cash Cat is for

Cash Cat is built for **everyday New Zealanders** who are not technical specialists. It is for people who want to **see where their money goes**, make **plans and budget goals**, and spend **less time each week or month** sorting through bank exports, PDFs, or several different tools.

## What we are trying to achieve

- **Less noise and effort:** Bring payments from **New Zealand bank accounts and cards** into one place—whether you connect accounts or import files—so you are not re-checking the same transactions in multiple apps.
- **Cut and slice your data:** Include or exclude **accounts** and **types of spending** so totals match how *you* think about your money, not how the raw bank feed is labelled.
- **Categories that make sense:** **Categorisation** is not a developer feature—it is how Cash Cat keeps filters, budgets, and charts trustworthy. Good categories mean you can answer “how much went to groceries?” without hand-editing every row.

## Privacy and control

Cash Cat is **local-first**: your data stays on your device in ordinary use (see the project README and install docs for how the desktop app and optional connections work). The goal is confidence that you are looking at *your* numbers, on *your* terms.

## How to use this document

Product copy, onboarding, and UX decisions in the app should be checked against this page: **plain language**, **next-step guidance**, and **no jargon** in default screens. Technical detail belongs in developer docs (for example [`categorisation.md`](categorisation.md)), not in the first experience for new users.
